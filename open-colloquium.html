Purpose

Objectives

  • Enable knowledge exchange between chairs at SWI and Saarland University.
  • Foster interdisciplinary discussion across methods, topics, and research cultures.
  • Create a forum in which PhD students can present their work.
  • Invite external researchers, including colleagues from other departments at Saarland University.
  • Provide an additional stage for extraordinary master students to present their work.

Format

Session profile

The colloquium is intended to be hosted preferably onsite in Saarbruecken, with remote participation generally possible so the format remains open and accessible across the institute's network.

Cadence

Rules

Presentation guidelines

  • The format is reserved for presentations by PhD students, researchers, or extraordinary master students.
  • Presentations should be concise, roughly 5 to 15 minutes, with at least an equal amount of time reserved for questions.
  • Multiple presentations can be held in one session, with a maximum of 3 talks.
  • Relevant presentations from the Chair of Sports Analytics may be presented in the internal colloquium first so feedback can be integrated.
  • All chairs are encouraged to suggest or submit contributions by adding presenter names and topics to the shared session planning list.
  • The final agenda for each session is arranged and shared by the meeting owner.

Value of the format

Why it matters

Because talks are intentionally short, the colloquium can surface more work-in-progress, create faster feedback loops, and make interdisciplinary exchange feel routine rather than exceptional.

Best use cases

What belongs here

The strongest contributions are focused research snapshots, method demos, early findings, or problem statements that benefit from cross-chair input without requiring a full-length seminar.
